Here are some black leaders in the realm of CS.

Gerald "Jerry" Lawson (1970's)



  • Jerry Lawson is an engineer that pioneered the concept of the video game cartridge.

  • Member of the "Homebrew Computer Club" including members like Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniack.
  • He created the first console with removable media. (Fairchild Channel F Console)

Roy L. Clay, Sr.
(1960's)



  • Roy L. Clay is a computer science pioneer who was dubbed the “Godfather of Silicon Valley.”

  • He led the team that engineered HP's entrance into the computer market with the development of the 2116A computer in 1966.
  • Clay Sr. has founded many organizations to promote the growth of computer science within the black community.

Katherine Johnson
(1950-80's)



  • Katherine studied how to use geometry for space travel. She figured out the paths for the spacecraft to orbit Earth and to land on the Moon.

  • Her efforts led to putting the first Americans on the moon. Katherine's job was so complexed her position was actually called a "computer"
  • Katherine Johnson worked for NASA for more than 30 years. She retired in 1986.


Mark Dean
(1980's)



  • Mark Dean is an engineer that created the ISA bus, and lead a design team for creating one of the first 1GHz processors.

  • He currently holds 3 of 9 patents for being the co-creator of the IBM personal computer released in 1981
  • Mark Dean was the first African American to be named an IBM fellow.

Clarence “Skip” Ellis
(1960's)



  • Clarence "Skip" Ellis was the first African American to earn a Ph.D in Computer Science!

  • He graduated from the University of Colorado at Boulder.
  • Skip Ellis was also the first African American to be elected a fellow to the Association of Computing Machinery (ACM)
